# Vemo — 2-Minute Demo Script

## [0:00–0:25] The Problem: A Longevity Crisis Hiding in Plain Sight

We talk about longevity as diet, exercise, sleep — but we're ignoring the place where health silently deteriorates for 8+ hours a day: **work.**

Post-COVID, remote workers spend 6–7 hours a day on video calls. Screen time is at an all-time high. And here's what the data says:

- **76% of workers** report at least one symptom of a mental health condition.
- **Burnout, depression, and chronic anxiety** are accelerating across every industry.
- These aren't just mental health issues — they directly attack the **five pillars of longevity**:
  1. **Sleep** — work stress is the #1 driver of poor sleep quality
  2. **Stress management** — chronic cortisol elevation from back-to-back meetings drives inflammation
  3. **Exercise** — meeting overload eliminates movement from the day
  4. **Nutrition** — stressed workers skip meals or stress-eat
  5. **Social connection** — paradoxically, people on 7 calls a day feel more isolated than ever

The worst part? These people are **silent sufferers**. They don't raise their hand. They don't tell HR. They just slowly decline — and we see it in the 6-month trend data.

## [0:25–0:50] The Solution: Vemo — Your Workplace Wellbeing Agent

Vemo brings together **voice biomarkers, wearable biometrics, and agentic AI** to detect and intervene — before burnout becomes a clinical problem.

Here's how it works:

**It runs silently in the background.** Vemo connects to your calendar, knows your meeting schedule, and can read meeting context you add — who's attending, what kind of meeting it is, what's at stake.

**It's present on every call.** Vemo records your meetings — not just for transcription and notes, but to run the audio through **state-of-the-art acoustic models**:

- **emotion2vec+** — converts speech audio into spectrograms and classifies 9 emotional states in real time
- **Kintsugi Large Acoustic Model (DAM 3.1)** — clinically validated voice analysis that correlates with **PHQ-9** (depression) and **GAD-7** (anxiety) screening tools

This isn't sentiment analysis on text. This is **voice as a vital sign** — the same way we treat heart rate or blood oxygen.

**It overlays wearable data** — heart rate, HRV, respiratory rate, recovery scores — to cross-reference what your voice says with what your body confirms.

## [0:50–1:20] How It Works Day-to-Day

**[Show employee select screen]**

Vemo tracks each employee across their day. You can see recovery scores, meeting load, and hours on calls at a glance.

**[Show day view / play mode]**

Throughout the day, Vemo silently monitors every meeting. It generates **daily reports, weekly trends, and 6-month trajectories** — mapping stress, mood, and recovery over time so you can see patterns before they become problems.

**[Trigger fires — alert pops up]**

But if something concerning happens mid-day — Vemo **intervenes in real time**. Right here, it detected emotional distress in this meeting. It pauses, checks in with the employee via a voice sample, runs it through the acoustic models, and returns clinical-grade scores.

**[Show results + skills]**

Then it activates its **Betterness Skills** — a protocol library purpose-built around the five pillars of longevity:

1. **Stress Resilience** — breathing protocols, nervous system regulation, immediate de-escalation
2. **Recovery Coach** — strain management, recovery optimization, workload balancing
3. **Sleep Analyst** — sleep debt tracking, evening wind-down protocols, circadian rhythm support
4. **Heart Health Scorecard** — HR/HRV analysis, cardiovascular strain alerts, movement prescriptions
5. **Inflammation Tracker** — chronic stress markers, inflammatory load monitoring, anti-inflammatory interventions
6. **Longevity Protocol** — comprehensive multi-pillar intervention for severe cases, connecting all five longevity pillars
7. **Morning Briefing** — proactive daily planning based on recovery, sleep quality, and upcoming meeting load

**[Show streaming agent response]**

Vemo doesn't just report — it **takes action**. It blocks your calendar. It starts a breathing exercise. It adjusts your strain targets. It's agentic — it does things, not just suggests them.

## [1:20–1:50] Long-Term Impact & the Longevity Connection

**[Show 6-month trends view]**

Over time, Vemo tracks the impact. You can see whether an employee is **declining, stable, or improving** across all five longevity pillars:

- Is their stress trending up? → Sleep and stress management pillars are failing
- Is recovery dropping? → Exercise and nutrition pillars need attention
- Are they isolating in meetings? → Social connection pillar is at risk

When patterns show sustained decline, Vemo escalates — connecting employees to **therapy, coaching, or clinical resources** as needed. Early detection, not late intervention.

## [1:50–2:00] The Business Model & Close

Vemo is offered to **employers as an employee benefit** — just like Spring Health, Lyra Health, Headspace for Work, or Modern Health. Companies already spend on EAPs that nobody uses. Vemo is different because it's **passive, continuous, and clinically grounded**.

The goal: **improve productivity and happiness over time** — not by working people harder, but by protecting the five pillars of longevity that make sustained performance possible.

Burnout isn't a productivity problem. It's a **longevity problem**. And Vemo is how we solve it.
